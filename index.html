Valrean Craftbeer.

by MALLOW ONESHOE

When I first walked into Valrum two things shocked me most. The impressive builds, and the copious amounts of alcohol. I found Russian Vodka just lying around! Russian Vodka that you wont be able to find anymore. I decided to explore some of the homebrewing alcohol scene in Valrum after this. The brews that aren't sold but shared among friends. Wheat Beer is probably the easiest and most accessible of alcohols to make. I found that among groups of friends this one is often drunk. Be it after a baptism or successfully gaining citizenship, Wheat Beer was drunk for all these occassions.

Some people are happy leaving it at Wheat Beer, and brewing a handful of bottles in between whatever else they do. But among these homebrewers is another breed, the artisan. These players experiment with recipies and workflows. Aiming for higher quality, more difficult to make drinks. They may dabble in distilling, exotic ingredients, and trying to achieve the highest alcohol content possible. These players also seem the most excited to share their creations. From giving away free bottles of Absinthe to anything being worth celebrating with a drink. They are truly a delight to spend time with.


In light of this I would like to try encourage Valreach's homebrew scene to grow to new heights! How I do this? I am not too sure. Perhaps fortnightly craftbeer meet ups? Brewing competitions? I may not have the answers but I would certainly like to try more homebrewed drinks.

Weekly Deepdive - Valriegeschlange

by REIL GILDEDTOUNGE

Valriegeschlange ist zie schlange bon Valrieges. Despite this, it is rarely spoken outside of formalities or traditions. This language does have a place in the common speak of our people, not only as a unifying aspect of our culture but as a way to discreetly communicate in the presence of other peoples. As such I have taken it upon myself to create a way of making the language more accessible. I have done this by creating a Valriegeschlange translator. This translator allows users to turn English into Valriegeschlange easily. You can access the translator here.

This translator does have some gaps and issues however. There are many words missing from Valriegeschlange that exist in English which can lead to some sentences needing to be a mix of both. To help address this I will be spending the next week working on creating a fork of Valriegeschlange that is useable in more situations. I also make a public statement to all citizens of Valreach. If we want our language to not just survive, but thrive, we need to create an Imperial Ministranum of Language. A legal body to oversee and enforce the growth and further use of this language. From transcribing treaties and legal documents to commissioning songs, this ministry could ensure the success of Valriegeschlange. If this sounds interesting to you help make it a reality!
